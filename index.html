📩 Submission Cover Letter

I am pleased to submit my manuscript titled "Breathing Membrane Quantum Mechanics: A Geometric Breathing Model of Quantum Evolution" for consideration and review.

This work introduces a novel nonlinear extension of quantum theory, grounded in the hypothesis that the fundamental fabric of reality is a breathing membrane evolving through an intrinsic temporal parameter—breathing time (τ)—rather than classical coordinate time. Quantum states are reinterpreted as breathing configurations ψ(τ, x), stabilized by a universal dynamical invariant: the Sionic Constant (σ ≈ 1.7365).

The paper establishes a unified framework connecting membrane geometry with quantum amplitude dynamics, entropy minimization with measurement collapse, entanglement with synchronized breathing across distant regions, categorical and gauge-theoretic formalisms, and quantum simulation tools via Qiskit-based encoding of membrane evolution.

Standard quantum mechanics is recovered in the small-ψ limit, but the framework predicts new behaviors in regimes where nonlinearity and coherence interact. Potential implications include refined interpretations of black hole information persistence, rhythmic origins of identity, and experimental deviations in coherence decay under weak measurement.

I have developed this theory independently over the last year, building bridges between quantum mechanics, geometry, thermodynamics, and quantum computing. The manuscript is written with rigor and clarity, and all key derivations, axioms, and simulations are presented explicitly.

I hope that this contribution can serve as a meaningful step toward rethinking the foundational architecture of quantum theory.
